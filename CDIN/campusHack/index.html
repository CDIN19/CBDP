About The Event

The CDIN recently launched a Campus Blockchain Development Programme (CBDP) in 2018 through its Nigeria Blockchain Alliance initiative by conducting a series of campus blockchain hackathon events. One of the objectives of the programme is to give the students early exposure to the career and business opportunities being presented by the Blockchain digital innovations thereby stimulating sustainable national development.

F.A.Q

  • What is Campus Blockchain Development Program?

    The CDIN recently launched a Campus Blockchain Development Programme (CBDP) in 2018 through its Nigeria Blockchain Alliance initiative by conducting a series of campus blockchain hackathon events. One of the objectives of the programme is to give the students early exposure to the career and business opportunities being presented by the Blockchain digital innovations thereby stimulating sustainable national development.

  • What are the Objectives of CBDP?

    1. Establishment of indigenous academic programmes in Cryptography & Distributed ledger Technology to support the local content policy of the government and reduce capital flight through foreign scholarships.

    2. Development of technical expertise and intellectual properties in Cryptography & Distributed ledger Technology that will support the local content policy by creating local markets and attracting revenue and investors from all over the world

    3. To promote research based academic institutions/curriculum that is able to support emerging industry in technology innovation. And many more

  • Are you bringing this program to my Campus?

    The programme was started in pilot phase involving a few higher institutions in Nigeria that includes Ahmadu Bello University, Federal University of Technology Akure, Kwara State University, Covenant University, and University of Nigeria, Nsukka. Over a thousand seven hundred (1,700) students were engaged during the events. Arrangement is being made with the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA) to cover Baze university and other higher institutions in the next phase of the programme and please feel free to reach to us if you want us to visit your university.

  • What was the result of the past event?

    The preliminary intra-school hackathon event that was carried out on the campuses of five (5) universities across Nigeria engaged over 1,763 participants and 38 teams. Below are the universities involved in the pilot and the outcomes of the exercises.

    1. Convenant University, (CU) Sango Ota, Ogun State

    2. Kwara State University, (KWASU) Kwara State

    3. Ahmadu Bello University, (ABU) Zaria, Kaduna State

    4. Federal University of Technology Akure, (FUTA), Ondo State

    5. University of Nigeria, Nsukka, (UNN), Enugu State.
